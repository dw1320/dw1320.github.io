Orange and Cardamom Blondies with Tahini

By Daniel Wagman | February, 2023
While brownies are an all-time classic, sometimes I think that they outshadow their equally rich and delectable vanilla-laden counterparts–blondies–to an unfair extent. Hopefully, with a crackly top, nutty tahini, warm cardamom, and fresh orange zest, my take on the treat will help demonstrate why they deserve a place in your kitchen repertoire.

Ingredients

  1. 1 ⅓ (160 grams) cups white whole-wheat flour
  2. ¾ teaspoon baking powder
  3. ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  4. ½ cup (100 grams) granulated sugar (and an extra 1 tablespoon for topping)
  5. Zest from 1 orange (optional)
  6. ½ teaspoon sea salt
  7. ¼ cup (60 grams) unsweetened applesauce
  8. ½ cup (100 grams) extra-virgin olive oil
  9. ½ cup (100 grams) brown sugar
  10. 1 ½ teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  11. 6 tablespoons (90 grams) tahini

Instructions

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Line an 8 by 8 inch pan with parchment paper and set aside. In a medium mixing bowl, add the all-purpose flour, baking powder, kosher salt, and ground cardamom and whisk until well-combined. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the ½ cup (100 grams) granulated sugar and orange zest until fragrant, about 30-60 seconds. Add the unsweetened applesauce, extra-virgin olive oil, brown sugar, and pure vanilla extract to the orange-sugar mixture and whisk until combined. Then, add the tahini and whisk just until combined. The mixture will thicken slightly. Add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients and fold together with a rubber spatula until combined. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly with the rubber spatula. If using the extra 1 tablespoon granulated sugar, sprinkle it now over the top of the batter. Bake for 25-30 or until done and the blondies are just set in the middle. Take care not to overbake.
